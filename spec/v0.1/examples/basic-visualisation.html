The Role of AI in Modern Education

Artificial intelligence is increasingly present in educational settings, from automated grading tools to AI writing assistants available to students at every level. This has created a genuine challenge for educators: how to assess student effort and understanding in an environment where AI can produce competent text on demand.

The most common institutional response has been to deploy AI detection software. The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence has transformed many aspects of our daily lives, and education is no exception to this trend. However, the reliability of these tools has been questioned by multiple independent studies.

Subsequently, the implementation of neural architectures trained on large text corpora has demonstrated a systematic tendency to misclassify writing produced by non-native English speakers as machine-generated, owing to distributional differences in syntactic patterns and lexical choices that diverge from native-speaker norms present in training data.

A more productive approach would shift the question entirely. Rather than asking whether a piece of text was produced by a human or a machine, we might instead ask whether we can verify the process by which the text was created. This is the principle behind TWFF.
